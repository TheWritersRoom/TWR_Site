Chapter 3 — The Antique Shop
Text selected · suggest a change?

The city spread before Elara like a wound that had never quite healed — all cracked pavements and dimly lit windows that flickered in the autumn rain, each one a small world unto itself, indifferent to the lives pressing against it.

She had walked this street a hundred times since the funeral, always at dusk, always alone. The habit had started as grief and hardened into ritual, the way all things do when we carry them long enough.

"You have to stop doing this to yourself," Marcus had told her that morning over cold coffee, his voice gentle but certain. She had nodded, the way she always nodded, and then put on her coat the moment he left.

The antique shop on the corner was still lit. Ferrante's. She had never gone in, though she'd stood at the window enough times to know the inventory by heart: a brass compass, three taxidermied birds under glass, a chessboard missing the black queen.
